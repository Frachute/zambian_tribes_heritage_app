Lozi Tribe

The Lozi people inhabit the western part of Zambia in the Barotseland region. They are known for their well-organized monarchy headed by the Litunga (King). Their language is Silozi, and their governance has historic roots in the Barotse Kingdom.

Their most iconic event is the *Kuomboka* ceremony, a royal move of the king from the flood plains to higher ground, marked with colorful paddling, music, and pageantry.
